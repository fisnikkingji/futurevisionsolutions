Safety

Safer Than France, Belgium, Sweden & the UK

That's not opinion - that's Numbeo's 2025 crime index. Kosovo carries the same US travel advisory as Germany, and Pristina scores nearly identical to Vienna on safety. The perception is 27 years out of date.

Hot take: Kosovo is safer than France, Belgium, Sweden, and the UK. That's not my opinion. That's Numbeo's 2025 crime index. I live here. The most dangerous thing about Pristina is that nobody lets you leave a dinner without three courses, two desserts, and enough espresso to power a small city. The safety perception is just outdated. A war ended here 27 years ago. That's the entire basis for it. Most people here were kids. Some weren't even born. But that one word still triggers something in people's brains. Something from a news segment they half-watched in high school. Same US travel advisory as Germany. Pristina scores almost the same as Vienna on safety. Nobody knows this because nobody checks. And that's the part I think about as a business owner. Every wrong assumption about this country is a competitive advantage for the people who bother to visit. Youngest population in Europe. German and English spoken natively. 10 percent corporate tax. Full timezone overlap with the EU. And a workforce with a hunger you can't train into someone. Kosovo isn't the country people think it is. Hasn't been for a long time. That perception gap is expensive. Just not for the people who are already here.
Talent & Cost

The Crown Jewel Every Outsourcing Guide Is Missing

ICT exports hit €347 million in 2024 - up 21% year over year, a fivefold increase in just four years. English and German spoken natively. Senior talent at a fraction of Warsaw or Lisbon rates.

Every European outsourcing guide in 2025 names the same three countries. They're all missing the strongest region on the map. I've read every outsourcing report published this year. Tech StaQ. Devico. Index.dev. They all define the "European Execution Corridor" as Poland, Spain, and Portugal. Good countries. Real talent. But nobody mentions the Balkans. Not one list. And that's a problem, because the data says the Balkans should be on every shortlist. Croatia just ranked #2 globally in the 2025 EF English Proficiency Index. Score of 617. That's higher than Germany. Higher than Poland at #15. Higher than Portugal at #6. North Macedonia scored #17. Bosnia came in at #21. These aren't scrappy emerging markets trying to learn English. These are countries where professionals operate at native-adjacent levels. Now look at the rates. Senior developers in Poland bill at USD 65-90/hour. Portugal runs $85-90. In the Balkans, comparable senior talent runs $45-80 depending on the country and specialization. That's not "cheap labor." That's the same quality band at 30-50% less. The Balkans sit in CET - a 3-4 hour morning overlap with the US East Coast. But the crown jewel is Kosovo. Youngest population in Europe. Median age of 32. A 10% flat corporate tax, lowest in the region. The Euro as currency - no exchange rate risk. ICT exports hit €347 million in 2024, up 21% year over year - a fivefold increase in four years. We run teams out of Kosovo. English and German spoken natively. Young, hungry professionals who don't job-hop every six months. Every outsourcing guide in 2026 will include the Balkans. The data is there. The blind spot is real.
Demographics

While Europe Ages, Kosovo Is Just Getting Started

France's debt is 113% of GDP. Italy's is 137%. Kosovo's is 17%. GDP grew 4.4% in 2024. Seventy percent of the population is under 35 - two hours from Berlin.

17% debt-to-GDP. Youngest population in Europe. That's not Germany. That's Kosovo. Germany has 163 occupations with worker shortages right now. EU migration to Germany dropped 67% last year. A government study found a third of EU workers already living in Germany are thinking about leaving. Italy is the oldest country in the EU. Median age: 49. Almost 25% of the population is over 65. Fertility rate: 1.1%. By 2050 they'll have 1.5 workers for every retiree. In 2000 it was 4. The EU's working-age population is projected to shrink by 25 million people by 2070. Eurostat's number, not mine. This is the economic backdrop in the countries people consider safe. Now here's Kosovo. Median age: 32. Seventy percent of the population is under 35. Government debt: 16.9% of GDP. GDP grew 4.4% in 2024. Corporate tax is a flat 10%. The currency is the euro. 542,000 Kosovars grew up in Germany. Another 250,000+ in Switzerland. They speak German at home. Walk through Pristina and you'll hear it at every other table. France's debt is 113% of GDP. Italy's is 137%. The UK is 94%. The US crossed 123%. Kosovo is at 17%. Fitch gave it a BB- credit rating with a stable outlook - the first sovereign rating in the country's history. Europe's biggest problem right now isn't capital or innovation. It's that the workforce is disappearing. And two hours by plane from Berlin, there's a country full of German-speaking 30-year-olds where the government takes 10% and owes 17%. The companies figuring this out in 2026 have a window. I don't think it stays this wide for long. Infrastructure

Faster Internet Than France, the UK, Italy & Spain

252 Mbps average. 70%+ 5G coverage. 96% internet penetration. Kosovo went straight to fiber and 5G - Speedtest's Global Index ranks it 17th in the world.

Kosovo ranks 17th in the world for fixed broadband speed. Ahead of France. Ahead of the UK. Ahead of Italy. Ahead of Spain. That's not from Kosovo's government. That's Speedtest's Global Index. Anyone can check it in 30 seconds. 252 Mbps average. Over 70% of the country covered by 5G. Internet penetration above 96%. And somehow the first question I get from every potential client is still: "But is the internet reliable there?" Every time. Without fail. Before talent. Before cost. Before anything else. I've taken video calls with clients in New York, London, and Munich from my apartment in Pristina without a single dropped frame. I had worse connectivity in three different US cities I've lived in. When you think about it, the reason is straightforward. Kosovo rebuilt its telecom infrastructure from scratch after the war. There were no copper lines from the '80s to work around. No patchwork systems from three different decades stitched together. The country went straight to fiber and 5G because there was nothing to retrofit. A lot of Western Europe is still running on networks that were laid before most people had email. The broadband data is public. The safety data is public. The cost data is public. The talent pipeline data is public. All of it one Google search away. But the perception hasn't changed in 25 years. I run client-facing teams from here. The calls are crisp. The connections are stable. I'm not asking anyone to take my word for it. I'm asking them to look up one ranking. The distance between what people assume about Kosovo and what's actually true gets wider every year. And for the companies that bother to check, that distance is the entire opportunity.
